Recovery Time Objective (RTO) Example

Understanding RTO in a Financial Company Scenario

Scenario: Recovery Time Objective (RTO)

Imagine a financial company processing online transactions with an RTO of 2 hours.

Normal Operations

The company constantly processes transactions and updates its database.

Incident Occurs

A significant system failure occurs, leading to an outage in transaction processing.

RTO Definition

RTO is set at 2 hours, indicating the maximum acceptable downtime for the system.

Recovery Process Initiates

The company works on restoring the system to full functionality within the 2-hour timeframe.

Data Loss Consideration

During the downtime, transactions made within the outage period are temporarily unavailable but not permanently lost.
